# Sports Markets

**Market structure:** Both binary (Will team X win?) and bucket (over/under ranges, margin of victory, player stats).

## Edge Sources
1. **Injury information** — earlier access to injury news = edge
2. **Line movement** — sharp sportsbook lines move before prediction markets
3. **Situational factors** — rest days, travel, back-to-back games
4. **Weather** — outdoor sports affected by wind, rain, temperature
5. **Referee/umpire tendencies** — measurable impact on scoring

## Key Principles
- Sportsbook lines are set by sophisticated models + sharp bettors
- Polymarket sports markets are often less efficient than sportsbooks
- The edge is in *speed* (getting info first) or *niche knowledge* (obscure factors)
- Volume and liquidity vary massively — major events only

## Timing
- Line opens: sportsbooks release lines, market inefficiency highest
- 24h before: most injury info is public, lines have settled
- Game time: live markets (if available) can have edge during games

## Common Pitfalls
- Sportsbook odds already price in most public information
- Don't assume Polymarket prices should match sportsbook odds (different vig)
- Weather effects are overrated for most sports, underrated for some (golf, baseball)
